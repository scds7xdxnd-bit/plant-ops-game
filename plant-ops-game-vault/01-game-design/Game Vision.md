# Game Vision

#### One-Line Pitch

A chemical process engineering decision game where the player reads a simplified Basis of Design or Design Basis Memorandum and converts it into early process design decisions for a specialty chemical plant.

#### Target Player

The first target player is a chemical engineering student. The secondary audience is junior process engineers and process design trainees.

The learning goal is to help the player understand how simplified design basis information becomes early process engineering decisions.

#### Player Fantasy

The player is a junior process engineer. They are not operating the plant yet and they are not doing detailed mechanical design. They are converting design basis information into a defensible early process design package:

- read feedstock and product specifications
- identify operational and site constraints
- interpret utility, environmental, safety, and code requirements
- select appropriate process sections and equipment decisions
- produce simplified PFD, datasheet, control, safety, and environmental choices
- receive senior engineer feedback during design review

#### Design Pillars

- Design basis documents drive engineering decisions.
- Chemical engineering concepts should become visible choices.
- The MVP should feel like a mission-based design review, not homework.
- The first plant must stay small enough to understand.
- Wrong answers should teach process judgment, not just punish memory.
- Easy, medium, and hard modes should add ambiguity gradually.

#### Target Experience

The game should feel like a process design challenge with clear progress, scored feedback, and unlockable plant sections. It should avoid becoming a dry engineering quiz while still respecting real chemical engineering constraints.

Design basis documents should be realistic enough to feel like engineering and simplified enough to solve in 5 to 10 minutes.

Senior engineer feedback should be firm, specific, and design-review style.
