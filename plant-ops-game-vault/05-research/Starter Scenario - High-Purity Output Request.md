# Starter Scenario - High-Purity Output Request

#### Summary

A strategic customer asks for a higher-purity product grade after their own process begins suffering from inconsistent input quality. Marketing sees a premium segment opportunity, but the existing plant was optimized for volume rather than tight purity control.

#### Customer Signal

- Customer: Anchor customer in a high-margin segment
- Segment: Premium industrial buyer
- Request: Higher and more consistent purity
- Pain: Their downstream process has higher scrap and rework
- Urgency: Contract renewal in one quarter
- Revenue potential: High if the grade becomes a premium offering

#### Marketing Brief

- Opportunity: Retain a strategic customer and open a premium product tier
- Strategic value: High
- Risk if ignored: Customer may shift volume to a competitor
- Proposed promise: Higher purity, tighter variability, same delivery reliability

#### Product Requirement

- Required output: Premium grade variant
- Quality target: Lower impurity range and lower batch-to-batch variation
- Delivery target: No reduction in contract service level
- Flexibility target: Ability to switch between standard and premium grades

#### Plant Context

- Plant type: Generic continuous or semi-continuous process plant
- Current bottleneck: Final separation or finishing unit
- Current reliability: Acceptable for standard grade
- Current quality issue: Variation spikes during changeover and high-throughput periods
- Current cost pressure: Energy usage and quality rework

#### Engineering Options

| Option | Cost | Time | Risk | Expected Impact |
|---|---:|---:|---|---|
| Add tighter final inspection | Low | Short | Low | Catches defects but increases rejection and rework |
| Slow the bottleneck unit | Low | Immediate | Medium | Improves quality but reduces throughput |
| Upgrade process control | Medium | Medium | Medium | Improves stability with moderate downtime |
| Add polishing capacity | High | Long | Medium | Improves purity and unlocks premium segment |
| Reject premium promise | None | Immediate | Business risk | Protects plant but may lose customer |

#### Outcome Rules

- If the player promises premium quality without plant changes, customer satisfaction may rise briefly but defect claims increase later.
- If the player slows production, quality improves but standard customers may experience delivery delays.
- If the player invests in process control, variability improves and operator burden increases during rollout.
- If the player adds polishing capacity, capital cost and project risk increase, but premium revenue becomes viable.
